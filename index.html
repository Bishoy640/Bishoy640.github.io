Egyptian civilization

The ancient Egyptian civilization was one of the oldest and most powerful cultures in history. It began around 3100 BCE along the fertile Nile River, where rich soil and steady floods allowed farming to thrive. Egyptians built great cities, created a writing system called hieroglyphs, and developed remarkable art, science, and architecture — including the famous pyramids. Religion played a central role in their lives, with hundreds of gods, grand temples, and strong beliefs about the afterlife. For over 3,000 years, Egypt remained a land of innovation, beauty, and mystery that still fascinates the world today.
